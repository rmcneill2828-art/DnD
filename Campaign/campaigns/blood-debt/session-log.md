## Session 21 — 2026-07-24

**Threnn's people arrive:** two days after Kaldur's fresh estimate, the watch sighted the column, and the whole community of roughly three dozen goliath-kin reached Urskelde's gates by midday. Threnn himself, greeting Darkhawk by his birth name, admitted he'd half-expected the promised place to turn out not real — and stood visibly moved to find it was. Settling in took the better part of two days: Joss handled housing with practiced ease; Perrin and Greta found instant kinship with Threnn's own smiths at the forge; Corren spent both days walking the glade's boundary with Threnn's hunters; Sael quietly took in a pair of Threnn's elders who began helping tend her grove without being asked; and one of Threnn's own trainers sought out Merrick unprompted, leaving him visibly steadier afterward.

**Vosk arrives:** riders from Thundertree reached Urskelde the following evening. Darkhawk greeted her with a full bow, which she brushed off in favor of taking his face in her hands the way she did the night his identity was confirmed. Seeing Urskelde whole — walls, chamber, cairns, Threnn's people already settling in — she called it "not just held ground, but something worth calling by a real name."

Darkhawk gave her the same grave account already given Kaldur: Kethrun, the ancient wound, the seal being tested since Duskgate, a worst-case timeline of months, and at least three other sealed sites of unknown condition. Vosk recognized her own cairn-verse for what it had actually been warning against all along — "what sleeps beneath stone does not dream kindly" — and pushed for the first council to convene within days rather than wait on full formality.

**House Corvane:** Tomas, Halvard, and Magistrate Kell arrived from Kessick's Ford three days after word reached them. Halvard brought the seal fragment's identification in person: the reconstructed wing-and-blade signet belongs to **House Corvane**, old Ravenshollow nobility, faded from public affairs for at least a generation. An estate reportedly still stands on Ravenshollow's northern edge, nominally occupied by a skeleton household — worth walking past in person when there's time. No living name attached yet, and Halvard was careful not to overclaim a link between Corvane and the Warden's bargain beyond both tracing back to the same city.

**The first council, held:** convened that evening in the half-finished council chamber — Vosk, Threnn and two of his elders, Kaldur, Tomas, Halvard, Kell, Mara, and Sael, with Urskelde's own guards and workers explicitly invited to stand in and be heard. Darkhawk opened by thanking Urskelde's ordinary workers by name-of-role first, before any grave business, and framed tonight as the fast first tier of the two-tier council plan, with the wider regional council still to follow.

He laid out the full account to the whole room at once — Kethrun, the wound, the timeline, the other sealed sites, Threnn's people as living proof of the cost — then raised the Ashpit/Corvane thread alongside it, naming his own suspicion the two might be connected. Halvard and Kell both counseled caution: a shared city isn't yet proof of a shared hand, worth pulling on but not yet worth naming as fact. Threnn agreed it was at least reason to watch the name closely.

**Questions from the floor**, guards and workers included, as promised:
- **Sera (gate watch)** asked what to do if the threat's agents arrive before the summons is answered. Darkhawk's answer: build, fortify, live — pointed the room at what they'd already built as reason not to let fear take that from them.
- **Borin (Threnn's elder)** asked about a real plan to check the other sealed sites. Darkhawk committed the council's combined reach (all goliath tribes being summoned) to locating and securing them, and Borin suggested asking Kethrun directly rather than assuming three is the whole count.
- **Joss (stores)** asked whether tonight changes day-to-day planning. Darkhawk turned it back on him — fight or flee, if the enemy were at the gate tonight — and Joss chose fight, and to keep building as planned.
- **Magistrate Kell** asked for a concrete timeline. Darkhawk set it firmly: **one week from tonight, the full regional council convenes** — matching what was already sent out in the original summons.
- **Bren (mason)** asked how Threnn's people's customs get honored without conflict. Darkhawk answered with a mason's own metaphor — build on a sound foundation rather than tearing down and starting over — which visibly landed with him professionally.
- **Mira (freed captive, 16)** asked whether people like her would be expected to fight or told to flee. Darkhawk asked what *she* wanted; she said fight, having spent years having things happen to her rather than choosing. He answered that the choice is hers alone to make either way — to fight for her home or help in other ways — and it visibly moved both her and several other freed captives nearby.
- **Wren (garrison quartermaster, 20 years)** asked who leads if Darkhawk doesn't come back. Rather than answer for the room, Darkhawk turned the deeper question back on it — asking whether he'd ever actually been named leader, or simply acted as one — and opened the floor for anyone to put their own name forward. Nobody did. Kaldur volunteered himself honestly as the practical continuity (not a ruler, "the one who knows where the grain stores are"), and Threnn and Vosk both framed the silence itself as the real answer: trust freely given and freely renewable, not a title. Wren accepted it plainly.

**Closing the council:** Darkhawk asked Vosk, Threnn, and Kaldur to coordinate with the other tribes and consult Kethrun directly to locate the remaining sealed sites, and closed by telling every hunter, miner, guard, and mason in the room they were the most important people in Urskelde and that he needed them, not just their labor. It landed hard across the room — Sera, Bren, Joss, and Wren all visibly moved.

**Walking the settlement:** rather than end the night there, Darkhawk walked every corner alone, deliberately giving space to anyone too nervous to speak up in the full chamber. An old net-mender thanked him quietly for her grandson's new place among the trainees. A young deputy asked honestly what happens to Vale long-term; Darkhawk gave him an honest "not decided yet" rather than a confident lie. At the grove's edge, Merrick admitted, unprompted, that some part of him still isn't sure he wouldn't want to kill Vale given the chance, and that nobody had asked him that directly since the truth came out. Darkhawk told him honestly he hadn't asked because he trusted Merrick to say so himself when it mattered — which seemed to land better than any reassurance would have.

**Ends the session:** Urskelde now hosting Threnn's entire community, Vosk and Kessick's Ford's leadership both present and briefed, a full regional council formally set for one week from tonight, a task delegated to locate the other sealed sites via Kethrun and the wider tribes, and a settlement that heard its leader mean it when he said he needed every one of them. Open threads carried forward: the Ashpit betrayer's identity and House Corvane's possible connection, the intended target's fate, the condition of Kethrun's other sealed sites, what's above Malphestor and how close it's gotten, and what the full regional council will decide in a week's time.
