# Blood Debt

**Type:** Homebrew
**Starting level:** 1
**Tone:** Gritty, personal, revenge-and-identity driven — mercenary work as the frame, a bigger conspiracy underneath. Combat should feel brutal and earned; roleplay moments (interrogation, trust, mercy) matter as much as fights.

## Premise
Darkhawk Blondin works the bounty boards of the border cities, taking mercenary contracts under his own code: protect the innocent, and justice isn't the law. He's really looking for two things — the people who betrayed him and left him for dead in the fighting pits, and any thread back to the mountain tribe he was stolen from as a child.

The campaign starts with an ordinary bounty job that turns out to have a loose thread connecting to the ring he escaped. Long-term arc: uncover who ran (or still runs) the gladiator syndicate, get his answer and his reckoning, and along the way piece together fragments of his stolen identity.

## Starting Hook
A bounty notice in a border town — a job that looks routine (skip tracer, missing persons, or similar) but the client, the target, or the payment turns out to trace back to the fighting-ring underworld Darkhawk thought he'd left behind.

## Setting Notes
- Border/frontier region — town under some nominal law, mountains within reach (tie to Darkhawk's lost tribe) and old trade roads (tie to where the ring's fighters got moved/sold).
- The gladiator syndicate is not fully destroyed — it likely still operates, quietly, under a legitimate-looking front.
- Law in this region is inconsistent and often for sale — fits Darkhawk's "justice isn't the law" ethos and gives room for morally interesting bounty work.

## Known Factions
- **The Ring** (working name) — the gladiator syndicate that raised and betrayed Darkhawk. Full structure, leadership, and current status: unknown, to be revealed through play.
- **Darkhawk's tribe** — a Goliath community somewhere in the mountains; identity and fate unknown, to be discovered.
- Local authority and bounty office in the starting town — TBD in session 1.
