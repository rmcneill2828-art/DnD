Modern clans, Thundertree included, are what came after that alliance scattered. **At least three other sealed sites exist from Kethrun's era, current condition unknown** — he's had no way to sense his kin-sentinels this whole time. Also raised: whatever's hunting the seal may not need an *existing* wound at all — a patient enough power could try tearing an entirely new one given time and inattention.

New permanent ability on his sheet: once per long rest, call on it in battle for a 1-minute boost (extra 1d6 melee damage + a frighten aura while raging). Kethrun called it a genuine alliance, the kind his own makers had and the world has since forgotten how to make.
- **A turning point for Darkhawk:** hearing the full scope of the threat laid out plainly, his dread resolved into calm acceptance rather than deepening — confrontation being certain isn't a weight, it's clarity. Named his guilt over the Thundertree dead honestly again, reframed as meaning rather than waste. Mara and Kethrun both recognized it as a real, lasting shift.

Mason **Torvald** stayed behind with Kethrun to learn the old ward-work properly; a full two-day joint ritual (Ysolde, guided by Kethrun, with Torvald's masonry and Darkhawk's bear-spirit presence anchoring it) left the Standing Ring's seal the strongest it's been in a century.

Mara's Primeval Awareness and Ysolde's Eyes of the Grave found four faint, non-hostile undead presences — spirits frozen mid-moment rather than truly dead, protected rather than destroyed during the same ancient war, waiting on a "return" that never came (the same story as Kethrun, told across a whole community).
- **The ritual hall:** murals depict the full history — the tear in the sky, the joint goliath-kin/primal-spirit war, and the deliberate mass-freezing as protection. A hidden dais with four pedestals (mirroring the Standing Ring exactly) and four empty vessels (bear, storm, stone, still water) was found needing a genuine offering from each of four primal-aligned people to complete. Sael's round trip to Kethrun confirmed the mechanism: sincerity is tested the same way the anchor tested Darkhawk; false or obligated offerings are dangerous, honest ones are what carried him through.
- **Mara, Sael, and Ysolde each made their offering, deliberated privately (Darkhawk stepped away to let it be their choice alone), and all three succeeded.** Mara offered her hard-won courage to face fear rather than avoid it (bonded to a spirit of still water); Sael offered the end of her self-imposed isolation (bonded to a spirit of stone); Ysolde offered her shift from scholarship into real action (bonded to a spirit of storm). All three gained new permanent abilities (see character sheets). The bear-spirit itself helped by "calling out" to its old kin on the party's behalf, though it couldn't compel an answer.
