# World State — Blood Debt

_Living tracker — active threads only. Full history lives in `session-log.md`; this file should always be quick to scan._

## Party
- **Location:** The Standing Ring's inner chamber, deep in the unmapped Greyhorn Range. Home base remains **Urskelde** ("the bear's shelter" in Giant — formerly Ashpit/the mine/"the Bear Cave"), stable enough to run without the party (Kaldur, plus deputies Idra and Torvin, hold it).
- **The Standing Ring, resolved (for now):** the four-person ritual walk opened a hidden stair beneath the central slab, descending to a sealed chamber. There the party met **Kethrun**, an ancient stone sentinel — goliath-kin-made, older than Thundertree's own history — set to guard **an ancient sealed wound** where something extraplanar once tried to force a foothold into these mountains, fought back by long-dead goliath-kin and primal spirits (kin to the bear-spirit) working together. Kethrun confirmed Urskelde's own boundary cairns are the "lesser ward" technique of the same ancient tradition — whoever built them was unknowingly continuing his makers' work.
- **The seal is thinning, and it's connected to the main threat:** Kethrun has felt something testing the seal faintly since the night Duskgate's anchor broke — very likely whatever sits above Malphestor, searching for a new foothold now that its old one (the Warden's bargain) failed. Closing this fully won't end that threat, but buys real time.
- **Darkhawk formally took Kethrun and the wound under Urskelde's protection** — asked for, not assumed. Kethrun accepted: he'll sense anything testing the seal and relay warning through the cairns if they're kept tended, in exchange for Urskelde sending people (a mason who understands old ward-work, a cleric to renew blessings regularly) to help him maintain the seal rather than leaving him to hold it alone. **Sael and Ysolde have gone back to Urskelde to gather help and bring Vosk directly; Darkhawk and Mara stayed at Kethrun's chamber to wait.**
- **Kethrun's history lesson:** an ancient, unified goliath-kin people once worked as true allies alongside primal spirits; the wound was a deliberate tear forced open by something patient and ambitious, driven back and sealed (not destroyed — none of Kethrun's makers believed true destruction possible) after a years-long war. Modern clans, Thundertree included, are what came after that alliance scattered. **At least three other sealed sites exist from Kethrun's era, current condition unknown** — he's had no way to sense his kin-sentinels this whole time. Also raised: whatever's hunting the seal may not need an *existing* wound at all — a patient enough power could try tearing an entirely new one given time and inattention.
- **The Bear's Answer:** Darkhawk fully opened himself to the bear-spirit and offered it a mutual, freely-given vow of partnership (not just gratitude) — accepted. New permanent ability on his sheet: once per long rest, call on it in battle for a 1-minute boost (extra 1d6 melee damage + a frighten aura while raging). Kethrun called it a genuine alliance, the kind his own makers had and the world has since forgotten how to make.
- **A turning point for Darkhawk:** hearing the full scope of the threat laid out plainly, his dread resolved into calm acceptance rather than deepening — confrontation being certain isn't a weight, it's clarity. Named his guilt over the Thundertree dead honestly again, reframed as meaning rather than waste. Mara and Kethrun both recognized it as a real, lasting shift.
- **Vosk and Kaldur brought reinforcements**, then returned to their own duties. Mason **Torvald** stayed behind with Kethrun to learn the old ward-work properly; a full two-day joint ritual (Ysolde, guided by Kethrun, with Torvald's masonry and Darkhawk's bear-spirit presence anchoring it) left the Standing Ring's seal the strongest it's been in a century.
- **The ancient goliath-kin village, found and partly restored.** Two days northeast of the Standing Ring per Kethrun's directions: an entire village carved into the mountainside, seemingly abandoned without any sign of battle, flight, or death. Mara's Primeval Awareness and Ysolde's Eyes of the Grave found four faint, non-hostile undead presences — spirits frozen mid-moment rather than truly dead, protected rather than destroyed during the same ancient war, waiting on a "return" that never came (the same story as Kethrun, told across a whole community).
- **The ritual hall:** murals depict the full history — the tear in the sky, the joint goliath-kin/primal-spirit war, and the deliberate mass-freezing as protection. A hidden dais with four pedestals (mirroring the Standing Ring exactly) and four empty vessels (bear, storm, stone, still water) was found needing a genuine offering from each of four primal-aligned people to complete. Sael's round trip to Kethrun confirmed the mechanism: sincerity is tested the same way the anchor tested Darkhawk; false or obligated offerings are dangerous, honest ones are what carried him through.
- **Mara, Sael, and Ysolde each made their offering, deliberated privately (Darkhawk stepped away to let it be their choice alone), and all three succeeded.** Mara offered her hard-won courage to face fear rather than avoid it (bonded to a spirit of still water); Sael offered the end of her self-imposed isolation (bonded to a spirit of stone); Ysolde offered her shift from scholarship into real action (bonded to a spirit of storm). All three gained new permanent abilities (see character sheets). The bear-spirit itself helped by "calling out" to its old kin on the party's behalf, though it couldn't compel an answer.
- **The village's frozen people are being released** — all four vessels filled, the whole hall (and reportedly the wider village) responding at once. Outcome of the full release not yet resolved — mid-scene.
- **Darkhawk and Mara are married**, not just engaged — a Thundertree rite plus Sister Ysolde's blessing, arranged in secret by Darkhawk as a surprise. (See character sheets for details.)
- **Duskgate is fully and completely resolved.** The anchor is broken, the Warden's bargain permanently ended, every soul bound to the altar freed. Market cleared, captives freed, strongroom wealth recovered (see character sheets for gold/gear — authoritative, not duplicated here).
- **Malphestor's fate is unknown** — stripped of purpose, not hostile, walked off to await judgment from its own superiors. Its parting words hinted at something further up the chain that felt every banked soul vanish when the anchor broke; nobody's confirmed what that is or whether it can reach this far.
- **The succession mechanic is the live security concern:** the Warden's bargain doesn't renew automatically on death — it requires a living successor to travel to Duskgate in person and accept it. Vale had been quietly grooming **Merrick** as that successor without ever telling him. As long as neither reaches Duskgate again, the bargain has no one to renew into — making Urskelde, which holds both of them, the single most important place to keep secure. (Contingency: if no successor arrives within a fortnight of a warden's fall, Malphestor is entitled to compel one directly — Merrick first, or failing that whoever else is vulnerable enough to bend.) **Wards now complete:** five clerics from Ysolde's order arrived with consecrated materials and finished warding both cells directly; neither prisoner has tested the doors since.
- **Resolved this session:** the two coded ledgers were decoded — written in Infernal, they turned out to be a partial copy of the bargain's own contract rather than separate shipping records. **Edmund Vale is the Warden himself** — no deeper hidden figure behind him. Darkhawk used the black scrying mirror to show himself to Malphestor directly; it recognized him as Ashpit's pit-fighter "number 7," was visibly thrown that he didn't match what its own ledger predicted, and invited him to Duskgate — the invitation that led to the final confrontation. **Thyra, Darkhawk's sister, has now been met** at Thundertree.
- Conditions/effects active: Darkhawk Barbarian 9/Fighter 4 (Battle Master, 117 HP), Mara Ranger 13 (Gloom Stalker, 86 HP), Sael Druid 11 (Circle of the Moon, 73 HP), Ysolde Cleric 12 (Grave Domain, 86 HP) — all full party members, all weapons consecrated against fiends. Darkhawk and Mara now proficiency +5 (character level 13); Sael and Ysolde remain +4. Darkhawk has permanent advantage on saves vs. charm/fear/possession (Bear-Claimed), a claw-shaped scar from the bear-spirit bond, and **The Bear's Answer** (a battle-boost callable once per long rest). Mara, Sael, and Ysolde each have a new primal-bond ability (Deep Water's Stillness, Mountain's Patience, Storm's Judgment respectively). Darkhawk's true name confirmed: Rurik, son of Sesha and Harek of Thundertree. (Full HP/gear: character sheets.)
- **New allies:** Joss, Idra, and Corren — former mine captives who chose to stay and fight rather than flee; fought through the barracks and processing area unharmed, now presumably training/settling at Urskelde.
- **Thundertree losses at the entrance:** three warriors dead (Ren Alderhawk, Bram and Torin Stonebrand), 8-9 wounded but recovering. Told in person by Rurik and Kaldur. Yorik Stonebrand gave Rurik a mourning-stone to carry into battle; Ren's widow Vessa and daughter Kessa asked only that he be remembered as a friend.

## Player Choices of Consequence
A running pattern, not a fixed rule: Darkhawk has moved between ruthless (killing surrendering guards at the mine-road ambush, no-quarter at the fairground building) and merciful (turning the waystation and first relay stop rather than killing, freeing captives with a genuine choice to flee or fight, taking Vale alive) depending on the moment and who's in front of him — usually erring toward mercy when someone stops actively fighting, but not always. **The defining choice of the campaign so far, at Duskgate:** rather than kill Malphestor, Darkhawk engaged it honestly, learned the anchor (not the devil) was the real target, and risked his own soul to break it instead — leaving Malphestor alive and telling it "if anger can fall, maybe devils can rise." Full blow-by-blow of each encounter is in `session-log.md`.
