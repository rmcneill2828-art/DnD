*(Mechanically, an Osyluth/Bone Devil — hell's own contract-inspectors, who exist specifically to verify infernal bargains are being honored. That's not a coincidence; it's exactly Malphestor's established role as the Warden's bargain's enforcer.)*

## Personality
- **Traits:** Patient, unhurried, frames everything in ledger and debt terms. Genuinely honest when honesty serves it better than a lie — "a devil that has to be honest with you is a devil that's found a truth dangerous enough not to need a lie" (Ysolde's read, confirmed).
- **Ideals:** The reckoning runs both ways. Cutting losses on a failing bargain and spending honesty now, while it still buys something, beats lying uselessly on the way down.
- **Bonds:** Recognizes Darkhawk specifically as Ashpit's "Number Seven" — the first debtor in its memory to close his own account in his own hand.
- **Flaws:** Uncertain, for the first time in its existence, whether redemption is possible for something like it — visibly moved by Darkhawk's answer ("if anger can fall, maybe devils can rise").

## Backstory
Malphestor served as the Warden's bargain's enforcer, presiding over Duskgate's inner chamber and the anchor that bound freed souls into the Circuit's machine. Confronted directly by Darkhawk, it chose a long, honest dialogue over a fight — confirming that killing it alone would change nothing (the office survives its holder), and that only destroying the anchor itself, at real cost to a willing soul, could end the bargain for good. When Darkhawk broke the anchor and freed every bound soul, Malphestor was stripped of purpose. Rather than wait passively for judgment, it chose — for the first time — to walk away on its own. Its fate since is unknown; it does not know itself whether its own superiors will arrive in moments or much later.
