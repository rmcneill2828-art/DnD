## Features & Traits

**Ranger**
- **Favored Enemy (Humanoids — the Circuit specifically):** advantage on Survival checks to track them, learn their signs/codes over time, and pick up a related language.
- **Natural Explorer (Forest):** her home ground — never gets lost except by magic, can travel stealthily at normal pace, finds double food when foraging, and gets extra information/advantage on initiative and first-round surprise attempts in this terrain.
- **Primeval Awareness:** can expend a spell slot to sense whether aberrations, celestials, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, or undead lurk within 1 mile (6 in favored terrain).
- **Fighting Style — Archery:** +2 to attack rolls with ranged weapons.
- **Spellcasting:** Wisdom-based. Spell save DC 13, spell attack +5. Known: **Hunter's Mark, Longstrider, Cure Wounds, Pass without Trace.** Slots: 4 (1st), 2 (2nd).
- **Extra Attack** (5th level).

**Gloom Stalker (Ranger Archetype)**
- **Dread Ambusher:** see Attacks above; also grants +WIS to initiative and +10 ft speed on the first turn of combat.
- **Umbral Sight:** darkvision 60 ft (or +30 ft if already possessed); invisible to darkvision-reliant creatures while she's in darkness or dim light.
- **Bonus spells (always prepared, don't count against spells known):** Disguise Self, Rope Trick.

**Outlander**
- Wanderer: perfect recall of terrain traveled, can always find food/fresh water in the wild for herself and up to 5 others, and find the way back to civilization.

## Personality
- **Traits:** Reads terrain and people the same way — patient, watches before acting, rarely the first to move. Dry, quiet humor that surfaces most when she's actually relaxed.
- **Ideals:** The people who go missing matter, not just the ones with someone rich enough to post a bounty.
- **Bonds:** The missing tannery lads who used to drink at her table — the reason the Dead Ground site was personal before Darkhawk ever showed up. Now, fully and openly, Darkhawk himself.
- **Flaws:** Avoided the Dead Ground site alone for two years rather than push past her own fear of it — capable of the same kind of avoidance again if something cuts close enough to home.

## Backstory
A trapper and local guide who's worked the country around Kessick's Ford for years, Mara knew the region's wrongness — the dead ground with no birdsong, the missing tannery lads — long before Darkhawk arrived looking for answers. Hired first as a guide, she's become the campaign's other half: the one who gets into places Darkhawk can't sneak into, holds a line with a bow when he can't be everywhere, and reads a room or a lock as well as he reads a fight.

What started as paid work became a real partnership, and — after Ravensmoor — something more. She's committed to standing with him at Thundertree when the time comes, whatever that costs either of them.
