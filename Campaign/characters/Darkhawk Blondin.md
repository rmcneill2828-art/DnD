## Personality
- **Traits:** Reads a room like an arena — instinctively clocks exits, weapons, and who's about to start something. Goes quiet and gentle around anyone weaker or more frightened than him.
- **Ideals:** Justice, not law. The strong exist to protect the weak, not exploit them — arenas and thrones run on the same rotten logic, and he's done serving either.
- **Bonds:** He will find whoever betrayed him and left him for dead, and make them answer. He needs to know who he was before the ring took him — tribe, family, name.
- **Flaws:** Deep, near-automatic distrust of authority, law enforcement, and anyone claiming power over others "for their own good" — it's gotten him into fights he didn't need. When he senses betrayal, rage can override judgment.

## Backstory
Taken from his tribe as a child, Darkhawk doesn't remember much of the mountains beyond fragments — cold air, a carved trophy he's kept ever since, a name that isn't the one the slavers gave him. He was raised in the gladiator ring, sold, traded, and finally trained into one of its best. He fought his way to the top of that brutal ladder — and when he refused to throw a fight for the house's profit, they made an example of him and left him for dead in the pit.

He didn't die. Now he works as a bounty hunter and mercenary, rootless, taking jobs across the region — but he runs by his own code, not his employer's: innocents get protected, no exceptions, and justice is not the same thing as the law. Somewhere out there are the people who betrayed him, and somewhere further back, in memory he can barely hold onto, is who he actually is.

**Identity confirmed (Session 12):** Reunited with Thundertree — his birth name is **Rurik**, son of **Sesha** and **Harek**, taken as a young child in a slaver raid that also killed two elders and a hunter. He has a younger sister, **Thyra**, born after the raid and not yet met. Still answers to "Darkhawk Blondin" day to day; the name Rurik is real but not yet fully his in practice.

**Converging thread:** Independent investigation into the Circuit's hidden central hub (as opposed to its known satellite sites) points toward the foothills of the Greyhorn Range — the same mountains associated with Thundertree territory. Mountain clans have reportedly grown more closed-off over the same period suspicious land purchases began there. The Circuit's true heart and Darkhawk's own lost clan may be on a collision course.
